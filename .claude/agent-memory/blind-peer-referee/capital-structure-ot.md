# Optimal-transport debt-capacity papers — referee checklist

Field: corporate finance / capital structure. Target tier claimed: JF/RFS/JFE.

## Recurring red flags to scrutinize
- **Circularity via Fréchet mean.** If the capital supply distribution nu is the Fréchet mean (cross-sectional average) of the firm distributions mu_it, then W1(mu_it, nu) is mechanically a "distributional outlier" score. Authors claim circularity is avoided because no leverage/spread data is used — but that only rules out one circularity. W1_shape may just proxy for being an unusual/distressed firm. Demand a horse race vs. distress proxies (Z-score, dist-to-default, ratings).
- **Sign instability across specs.** Headline W1->leverage coefficient flips sign as FE/controls are added (raw −0.25, +0.16 w/ moments, ~0 w/ controls, −0.07 w/ firm FE, −0.09 w/ firm+year FE). Within-firm negative is presented as "the test"; positive cross-sectional coefficient gets a post-hoc story. Treat fragile.
- **Claimed overidentification / cross-equation test never executed.** Intro sells a cross-equation restriction (same theta fits leverage AND spreads) as the key contribution; the estimation appendix describes a 31-moment SMM with Hansen J — but the actual results are a two-stage CALIBRATION (Fréchet mean OLS + spread slope), no J-stat reported, spread^model column blank in the fit table. The selling point is not delivered.
- **Weak structural fit dressed up.** Spearman rho ~0.44 with p=0.20 (insignificant), decile R^2 ~0.11, model misses top decile badly. Authors lean on the reduced-form panel instead.
- **Compustat oancfy is YTD.** oancfy/atq used "directly" must be de-cumulated to quarterly flows; otherwise the cash-flow distribution mixes Q1..Q4 accumulation/seasonality. Always ask whether this was done.
- **Document-state hygiene.** This series has shipped drafts with literal placeholders in conclusion ("[KEY EMPIRICAL FINDING TO BE ADDED]") and data appendix ([TABLE], [DESCRIBE]); inconsistent N (621,705 vs 698,466) and data-source naming (TRACE vs Mergent FISD) between abstract/intro/data. Check cross-section of numbers.

## Calibration
- A theoretically elegant new object (W1 debt capacity = half Wasserstein distance) is genuinely novel and worth a high Contribution sub-score, but do NOT let novelty inflate Identification/Data scores when the empirical test is fragile and the headline structural test is unexecuted. Recommend Major Revisions, not Reject, when the theory is sound and the gaps are fixable.
