# Literature Review — Recurring Patterns (Mode 1, Lit Critic)

## Coverage gaps that recur in structural / OT corporate-finance lit reviews
- **Matching / two-sided sorting in credit markets** is the gap most often missed when a paper's
  mechanism is distributional sorting (e.g., optimal-transport debt capacity, quantile coupling).
  Referees treat matching-with-transfers (Galichon lineage; Sorensen 2007 JF) as the closest
  neighbor — if absent, the novelty claim is under-defended. Penalize -15 (High) at Discovery.
- **Applied distributional / quantile regression** (Chernozhukov-Fernández-Val-Melly 2013;
  distributional regression) is often omitted even when Koenker-Bassett and CGHH (2017) ARE cited.
  Abstract OT theory present, applied estimation toolkit missing. -10 (Medium).
- **Recent structural estimation (post-BLP / post-2014)** frequently stops at Hennessy-Whited and
  Nikolov-Whited. Push for 2018-2024 frontier (Catherine et al. 2022 JF; Crouzet debt structure;
  DeMarzo-Sannikov security design). -8 (Medium).

## Process notes
- Watch for entries flagged "% UNVERIFIED" — treat as a credibility issue, deduct ~-5, and
  require verify-or-delete. Placeholders must not ship. Often also missing a BibTeX entry.
- Asset-pricing/returns papers (e.g., Gomes-Schmid "Levered Returns") get over-scored on proximity
  in capital-structure reviews; their link to *debt capacity* is indirect. Flag, don't remove.

## Scoring calibration
- A well-organized structural corporate-finance bib with strong OT foundations, all-top-5 journal
  mix, and complete BibTeX typically lands ~78 on first round — just under the 80 threshold —
  with the deficit concentrated in matching + applied-distributional + recent-structural strands.
  These are targeted-search fixes (88-92 achievable), not rebuilds.
- "Working-paper over-reliance" penalty rarely triggers in finance bibs that draw on JF/JFE/RFS;
  the failure mode is missing strands, not unpublished-paper bloat.
- CONFIRMED (distributional-debt-capacity review, 2026-06): a single targeted revision round filling
  the three strands (matching + applied-distributional + post-2017 structural) plus a verified-citation
  fix moved 78 -> ~91. The fixes were additive (new Categories 10/11) and properly back-integrated into
  the Frontier Map and Positioning sections — that back-integration is what earns full credit, not just
  appending entries. When checking a revision, verify the new strands appear in the synthesis sections,
  not only the annotated list. Watch for assortative-matching papers (Shimer-Smith, Choo-Siow) scored a
  touch high on proximity (3/5) — they are foundational, not direct neighbors; flag, don't penalize.
