\section{Numerical illustration}
\label{sec:numerical}

We now illustrate the quantitative implications of the model using the calibrated parameters described in Section~\ref{sec:calibration}. This section uses the baseline parameters: \(\Sharpe=0.35\), \(r_f=3.70\%\), investor risk aversion \(\gamma=5\), manager risk aversion \(\eta=3\), scale parameters \(A_0=1\) (normalized) and \(f_1=1.5\), and convexity \(f_2=25\).

Figure~\ref{fig:flow-performance} shows the key economic input to the model: flows accelerate after good outcomes. This is the core agency friction because it makes upside states disproportionately valuable to the manager.
Figure~\ref{fig:ic-fee-curve} shows the IC mapping implied by the model: for each target volatility $\sigma_d$, there is a fee level that makes the manager willing to choose it. Higher convexity shifts the schedule, reflecting stronger incentives to over-risk when performance chasing is more pronounced.
Figure~\ref{fig:opt-sigma-vs-f2} shows the equilibrium implication: as convexity increases, the investor optimally targets a lower volatility because sustaining a high target becomes more costly in terms of incentive alignment.
Finally, Figure~\ref{fig:affine-approx} assesses the quality of the affine approximation \eqref{eq:phi-linear-approx} introduced in Section~\ref{sec:manager} across different convexity levels. Notably, the average absolute error remains almost unchanged at around 0.1\% as the convexity parameter $f_2$ varies, demonstrating that the approximation quality is robust to more convex sensitivities of flows to performance. This stability confirms that the linear contract is an accurate and practical representation of the optimal incentive-compatible fee schedule across the empirically relevant range.

These results highlight how the model translates observable features of investor behavior into implications for contract design. For both investors and managers, these relationships provide a transparent mapping from a volatility target to a corresponding fee level, sustaining incentive alignment under realistic market conditions.
