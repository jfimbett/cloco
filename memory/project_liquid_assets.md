**Paper title:** Liquid Assets

**Authors (alphabetical by last name):**
- Juan F. Imbet, Paris Dauphine University - PSL
- Roman Kräussl, Bayes Business School, City St George's University of London
- Ilaria Piatti, Queen Mary University of London
- Roberto Steri, University of Luxembourg

**Core finding:** Bimodal price-age relationship in French wine auctions. Prices rise with age → fall past maturity → rise again. Conjecture: two buyer types — consumption buyers (value drinkability) and collector/investor buyers (value collectible status, unconcerned with maturity).

**Data:**
- French wine auction data (cleaned by multiple RAs — some quality concerns, see RA notes)
- Tastingbook data (being scraped) to infer wine maturity dates
- Plan: fit econometric model to estimate maturity for wines not covered by tastingbook

**Project type:** Structural + heavy reduced-form empirics. Estimation vs. calibration TBD.
